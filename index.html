My research focuses on Trustworthy AI, Mobile Computing, and Cybersecurity. I apply AI/ML to explore the use of ubiquitous modalities, such as WiFi signals, cameras, and acoustics, for developing novelty applications to protect human personal sensitive data and secure the operations of AI systems. I collaborate with professionals across diverse domains, such as Civil Engineering, Sociology, Health, and Data Science. I earned my PhD degree in Computer Science from North Carolina State University in 2023. During my PhD study, I had worked in the Wolfpack Interactive, Sensing, and Networking Lab with Prof. Muhammad Shahzad. I was a member of Wolfpack Security and Privacy Research Lab under guidance from Prof. Anupam Das. I currently lead the X+ Lab. I teach undergraduate students programming, software engineering, and cybersecurity courses at UNCP.

Research Interest:

  • Security and Privacy: fingerprinting and human biometric security, with a focus on safeguarding privacy and authentication through embedded sensors in digital devices.
  • Trustworthy and Explainable AI: designing context-aware mobile systems to bridge CPS and AI for reliable and adaptive interactions.
  • Mobile Computing: sensing with ubiquitous modalities, such as WiFi, camera, IMU, VR, UAV, and sound in intelligent transportation and smart homes.
  • LLM and NLP: speech privacy, speech translation, and LLM applications.
